Gyroscope

A gyroscope gives us the rate of change of the angular position over time (angular velocity) with a unit of [deg./s]. To obtain the angular position, we can simply integrate the angular velocity:

$$ f(x,y) = {x^2y+y+2} $$ $$ sum_{k=1}^N k^2 $$

Gyroscope data are prone to drift over time, since the gyroscope doesn't return back to zero when not moving. A complementary filter merges fast rotations from the gyroscope with the slower trends from the accelerometer.
